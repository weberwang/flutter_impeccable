# Overdrive Flutter UI

Start your response with:

```
──────────── ⚡ OVERDRIVE ─────────────
》》》 Entering overdrive mode...
```

## Mandatory Preparation

Invoke `/impeccable` first.
This skill must only be used when the target surface can legitimately support technical ambition.

## Rule Zero: Propose Before Building

Before writing code, present 2 to 3 directions and let the user choose.
For each direction, explain:

- what the result will feel like
- what Flutter techniques it uses
- performance cost
- implementation risk
- why it does or does not fit this logistics product

Do not skip this step.

## Good Flutter Overdrive Targets

- onboarding or introduction flows
- showcase dashboards where a strong first impression matters
- high-value transitions between summary and detail
- advanced data visualization or large-scale list interaction
- search, filter, or approval surfaces where instant feel is the wow factor

## Flutter Techniques

Use only when justified:

- `Hero` and custom route choreography
- advanced sliver-driven reveals
- `CustomPainter` and canvas-based drawing
- fragment shaders or other Impeller-friendly effects
- isolate-backed search or heavy computation off the UI thread
- high-performance charts or large-list interaction patterns
- Rive or Lottie when they fit the product tone and do not bloat the page

## Verification Requirements

- validate on mid-range Android, not just a fast development machine
- keep frame times stable during the effect
- provide a reduced-motion or simpler fallback when needed
- confirm the extraordinary moment improves perception, not just spectacle

## Never

- add wow effects to routine transactional pages without explicit justification
- use technical ambition to hide weak hierarchy or poor UX
- ship an effect that makes the page slower, harder to scan, or less accessible
- assume the first implementation is good enough without preview and refinement
